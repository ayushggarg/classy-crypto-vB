Cryptography

Cryptography is the practice and study of techniques for secure communication in the presence of third parties called adversarie. These adversaries are often referred to as Eve in cryptography, while the sender and recipient of messages are called Alice and Bob respectively. More generally, cryptography is about constructing and analyzing protocols that prevent third parties or the public from reading private messages, various aspects in information security such as data confidentiality, data integrity, authentication, and non-repudiation are central to modern cryptography.
