Affine Cipher



The Affine cipher is a monoalphabetic substitution cipher and it can be the exact same as a standard Caesarian shift when "a" is 1. Mathematically, it is represented as e(x) = (ax + b) mod m. Due to only 312 possible keys, this cipher is unsecure.




How to use the above tool?

To encrypt or decrypt enter your plaintext or ciphertext respectively. Chose a desired Key key 1 from the dropdown such that it is always coprime to length of the alphabet and then chose Key key 2. Press the encrypt or decrypt button accordingly.
