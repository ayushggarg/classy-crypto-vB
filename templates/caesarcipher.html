Caesar Cipher



The Caesar cipher is one of the earliest known, simplest and very weak cipher. It is a type of substitution cipher in which each letter is 'shifted' a certain number of places down the alphabet.
For eg., with a shift of 1, A would be replaced by B, B would become C, and so on.
The method is named after Julius Caesar, who apparently used it with a shift of three to communicate with generals to protect messages of military significance.
